Intruder Threats in Cybersecurity

What is an Intruder?

An intruder is an unauthorized individual who gains access to a system with malicious intent. These actors bypass authentication mechanisms to exploit data, disrupt operations, or cause damage.

Categories of Intruders

1. Masquerader

Masqueraders impersonate legitimate users using stolen credentials or social engineering.

2. Misfeasor

Insiders who misuse their access to perform unauthorized actions.

  • Example: An employee accessing confidential files without permission.

3. Clandestine User

Users with advanced privileges who manipulate system controls to stay hidden.

  • Example: A system admin installing a secret backdoor for persistent access.
