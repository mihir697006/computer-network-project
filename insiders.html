Insider Threats

What is an Insider Threat?

An insider threat involves someone from within the organization—like an employee, contractor, or business partner—who has authorized access but uses it to harm the organization, either intentionally or accidentally.

Types of Insider Threats

1. Malicious Insider

  • Deliberately steals confidential information.
  • Installs malware or sabotages systems.
  • Shares company secrets with competitors or cybercriminals.

2. Careless Insider

  • Falls for phishing attacks or uses weak passwords.
  • Leaves devices unlocked or unattended.
  • Accidentally leaks information via unsecured platforms.

3. Mole (Insider Impersonator)

  • Infiltrates the organization posing as an employee or contractor.
  • Acts on behalf of a competitor, nation-state, or cyber gang.
  • Plants surveillance or backdoors for future exploitation.

Preventing Insider Threats

  • Access Controls: Limit user permissions based on roles.
  • Employee Awareness: Regular security training and phishing simulations.
  • Monitoring: Use of activity logging and behavioral analysis.
  • MFA: Enforce multi-factor authentication for sensitive systems.
  • Incident Response Plan: Have a defined protocol for detecting and responding to threats.
